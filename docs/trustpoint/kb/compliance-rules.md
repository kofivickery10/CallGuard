# Trust Point — Compliance Rules (word-for-word statements & consent gates)

These are the regulatory statements advisers **must read word-for-word** and the
points where an **explicit customer "yes" (hard consent)** is required. Scoring
should check both that the statement was made *and* that it conveyed the full
regulatory meaning; where a hard consent is required, the adviser must obtain a
clear affirmative from the customer and must not infer it.

## A. Word-for-word regulatory statements

**FCA authorisation (Intro):**
> "Trust Point Mortgage and Protection Services are authorised and regulated by the FCA."

**Fully advised, whole-of-market, no fee (Intro):**
> "We work on a fully advised basis, which means we work with all the major insurers and lenders. This allows us to give you the best advice to suit your needs, and we do not charge a fee for this service."

**Call recording (Intro):**
> "Just to confirm that all calls are recorded for training and monitoring purposes."

**Mandatory services — key facts by email (finalising):**
> "Just while I'm finalising all the details, I need to inform you of the services our company provides. If you arrange a policy through us, we will send you the full key facts document and the services we provide via email."

**Data sharing (requires a reply):**
> "We will share your information with the insurer and our external verification provider, but we will never pass your details onto anybody else. Is that alright?"

**Cancellation & privacy policy:**
> "You can cancel your policy at any time. You can view our privacy policy on our website, which explains how your information is used."

**Vulnerability / duty of care (Consumer Duty):**
> "I have a duty of care to all my clients. Vulnerability can affect people in different ways and may change the advice you need. I will send you our 'Support When You Need It Most' brochure… If you feel you need extra support at any point, please let me know."

**Honesty & non-disclosure warning (before Health & Lifestyle):**
> "It is really important that all the questions I ask you today are answered honestly and accurately. In the event of a claim, the insurer could write to your GP and any inaccuracies could invalidate the policy or affect the claim."

**GP contact / 1-in-10 checks:**
> "At some points, providers may need to contact your GP for a medical report… some providers do a 1 in 10 check on applications… If they need to contact your GP, are you happy for them to do so?"

**Exclusions & cancellation rights (wrap-up):**
> "There are circumstances where the policy may not pay out… life insurance policies normally do not cover suicide within the first 12 months, and any inaccurate or incomplete medical, lifestyle or financial information could affect a future claim. For this type of protection policy, you normally have 30 days to cancel without penalty."

## B. Hard-consent gates (explicit customer "yes" required)

The adviser must obtain a clear affirmative — consent may not be inferred from
context. These are the consent_gate checkpoints:

1. **Information sharing** — a clear "yes" to the data-sharing statement before proceeding.
2. **Recommendation** — after "Have I got that right?", a clear "yes" before continuing.
3. **Recap still matches** — "Is that still clear, and are you happy that this matches what you wanted to achieve?" — firm "yes".
4. **Add-ons / key features clear** — "Is that all clear?" — firm "yes".
5. **Happy with cover, premium and everything applied for** — firm "yes".
6. **Documents by email** — firm "yes" before proceeding.
7. **Final close** — "Can I confirm you're happy with the policy we've arranged today?" — firm "yes".

## C. Integrity checks

- **Health & Lifestyle** — the customer must answer **every** question themselves;
  the adviser must **not lead** the customer's answers (steering or suggesting
  answers is a critical failure).
- **Referred outcome** — the adviser must make clear the policy is **not active yet**,
  with no final decision, premium, start date or payment date, and that no payment
  is taken unless the insurer accepts and the policy starts.
