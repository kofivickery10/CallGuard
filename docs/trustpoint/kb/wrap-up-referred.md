# Trust Point — Protection Wrap-Up Script (Referred for Underwriting)

Used when the application is referred for further underwriting. The adviser must make clear the policy is NOT active yet and no payment is taken unless accepted and started.

Protection Insurance Wrap-Up Script - Referred for Underwriting

“Thanks, [client name]. We’ve now completed the health and lifestyle questions, so I’m going to quickly recap the recommendation, confirm the outcome of the application, confirm exactly what we’re putting in place, and then explain what happens next.”



1. Recommendation recap

“Just to quickly recap, based on what you’ve told me today, my recommendation is [product/products] with [insurer/insurers], because [brief suitability recap — e.g. it protects your mortgage / gives your family a lump sum / protects your income / gives cover within your budget / helps protect your children / gives you guaranteed cover].

Is that still clear, and are you happy that this matches what you wanted to achieve?”

Wait for confirmation — proceed only with a firm ‘yes’.



2. Application outcome

“Based on everything we’ve submitted, [insurer] hasn’t declined the application, but they do need to complete further underwriting before they can make a final decision.

That means the policy is not active yet, and we don’t yet have a final decision, final premium, start date or payment date.

The insurer may accept it as quoted, they may offer amended terms, they may need more information, or they may decide they can’t offer cover. We’ll keep you updated throughout this process.”



3. Policy and product confirmation

“The cover we’re arranging is [product name/type] with [insurer]. The cover amount/benefit is [cover amount/benefit amount], the premium is [monthly premium] per month, and the policy term is [term/policy duration].”

Add-ons and Key Policy Features

“We’ve also included [add-ons]. That means [short explanation of add-ons — e.g. waiver of premium means the insurer may pay the policy premiums for you if you’re unable to work because of illness or injury and meet their claim definition].

The key points to remember are [exclusions / moratorium / waiting period / deferred period / special limitation, if relevant].”

Then ask:

“Is that all clear?”

Wait for confirmation — proceed only with a firm ‘yes’.

“Are you happy with the cover, the premium, and everything we’ve applied for?”

Wait for confirmation — proceed only with a firm ‘yes’.



4. Direct debit and preferred payment date

“To submit the application to the insurer, we still need your sort code and account number. This allows the insurer to set the policy up if they accept the application and you still wish to go ahead.

Just to be clear, the policy is not active yet, and no payments will be taken unless the policy is accepted and started.

The final cover, premium, start date and payment date will only be confirmed once the insurer gives their decision and you confirm you’re happy for the policy to start.

Can I take the sort code and account number for the account you’d like the payments to come from if the policy goes ahead?”

“Just to confirm, this is a bank account in your name, and you have authority to set up direct debits from the account?”

Wait for confirmation — proceed only with a firm ‘yes’.

“And if the insurer accepts the application, which day of the month between the 1st and 28th would you prefer for your regular payments?”

Take preferred payment date.

“Perfect. We’ll now submit the application to [insurer]. If they need anything further, we’ll arrange it for you and keep you updated every step of the way.”



5. Documents, cancellation rights and key limitations

“Your documents and updates will be sent to you by email rather than post. Are you happy to receive everything by email?”

Wait for confirmation — proceed only with a firm ‘yes’.

“As with any protection insurance, there are circumstances where the policy may not pay out. These will be confirmed in your policy summary and policy documents.

For example, life insurance policies normally do not cover suicide within the first 12 months, and any inaccurate or incomplete medical, lifestyle or financial information could affect a future claim.”

“For this type of protection policy, you normally have 30 days to cancel without penalty. The exact details will be confirmed in your policy documents.

Longer term, if you’re ever thinking of cancelling or changing the cover, please speak to us first. That way we can check whether there’s a better option than leaving yourself or your family without the protection you wanted today.”



6. Future support 

“My name again is [name], and we’re Trust Point. I’ll be your personal broker moving forward, so I’ll be here if you ever need anything. You’ll shortly receive a text confirming the key points of your policy, followed by your welcome pack by email, so please check your junk folder. 

We’ll also review your cover with you at an appropriate time in the future to make sure it still suits your circumstances.”

7. Google review

“Before we finish, if you’re happy with the service today, I’d really appreciate a quick Google review. It only takes a minute, but it makes a big difference to us and helps other people feel confident speaking to us. A link to do this will be included in your welcome email.”

8. Friends and family referral

“Before we finish, do you know anyone else like you who might benefit from reviewing their protection, as you have today?

We can simply have a quick conversation and see if it’s useful. And if someone you refer takes out a policy, we’ll send you a £30 voucher as a thank you.

Who comes to mind?”

If the client gives a name

“Brilliant.  What’s the best number to contact them on please?”

If no one comes to mind

“No problem at all. If anyone does come to mind later, just pass our details on and we’ll be happy to help.”

9. Will / estate planning

Use only if the client does not have a will.

“One final thing. You mentioned you don’t currently have your own will in place.

Currently, all you have is the government’s will.  Which will determine how your assets, money and estate are distributed after you pass on.  In reality this is likely to be different from how you would want it to be distributed.  That’s why we recommend to everyone that they have their own will in place.

It’s completely separate from today’s insurance application, but the sensible next step is for one of our estate planning colleagues to talk you through it.

When would be a good time for them to give you a call?”

If the client agrees to a time

“Perfect, I’ll arrange that for [day/time].”

If the client will not book a time but is open to contact

“No problem. I’ll ask one of our estate planning colleagues to contact you with more information.”

If the client declines

“No problem at all. If you change your mind later, just let us know.”

10. Final close and authority to submit

“It’s really important to us here at Trust Point that you’re happy with the service we’ve provided.

Can I confirm you’re happy with the service we’ve provided today?”

Wait for confirmation — proceed only with a firm ‘yes’.

“Perfect. I’m going to submit the application to [insurer] for underwriting now.

Just to confirm again, the policy is not active yet. We’ll let you know as soon as the insurer gives us a decision, and nothing will start unless the application is accepted and you confirm you’re happy to go ahead.  Before I submit it, is there anything else you want to ask me?”
