# Trust Point — Sales Process v4 (June 2026)

The end-to-end advised sales process advisers follow before the wrap-up. Regulatory statements marked "MUST BE READ WORD FOR WORD" are scored as word-for-word checkpoints.

Trust Point Protection Services Sales Process



1. Intro

Aim

Build trust, explain who we are, and set up the call properly.“Hi [client name], it’s [adviser name] calling from Trust Point Mortgage and Protection Services.

You recently asked us to help you look at protection insurance, so I’m just calling to understand your situation properly and see what cover may be suitable for you.”[Use the intro from the relevant occupation script if applicable.]  

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE!  MUST BE READ WORD FOR WORD!

“Perfect, you’ve come to the right place, Trust Point Mortgage and Protection Services are authorised and regulated by the FCA.

We work on a fully advised basis, which means we work with all the major insurers and lenders. This allows us to give you the best advice to suit your needs, and we do not charge a fee for this service.

Just to confirm that all calls are recorded for training and monitoring purposes.

“I’ll ask some questions about your circumstances, financial commitments, income, family situation, and any cover you already have. Once I understand that properly, I can explain what protection may be suitable, what options are available, and what it would cost.”



2. Fact Find

Aim

Build a clear picture of the client’s risks, responsibilities, existing cover, affordability, and priorities.

Keep it conversational. Do not just complete a form.





Cover ALL of the following key areas:

Personal Client Information

Date of Birth

Address – including date moved in

Smoking status

Nationality

Household and dependants

Partner/spouse/children or other dependants. - Who relies on the client financially?

Any support needs or potential vulnerability.

Mortgage, debts and commitments

Mortgage details - balance and term.  Repayment or interest-only.  Joint or sole mortgage / rent details

Essential monthly commitments.

Will/Estate Planning

Income, employment and sick pay

Employment status and occupation.

Intended age of retirement

Income and partner income.

Sick pay.

Existing cover

Details of any existing cover, e.g. existing insurance or work related benefits



3. Mandatory Services / Vulnerability / Contact Statement

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE!  MUST BE READ WORD FOR WORD!

“Just while I’m finalising all the details, I need to inform you of the services our company provides.

If you arrange a policy through us, we will send you the full key facts document and the services we provide via email.

We will also put your policy into Trust free of charge should this be applicable.

We will share your information with the insurer and our external verification provider, but we will never pass your details onto anybody else. Is that alright?”

YES/NO — MUST GET A REPLY HERE.

“You can cancel your policy at any time.  You can view our privacy policy on our website, which explains how your information is used.

I have a duty of care to all my clients. Vulnerability can affect people in different ways and may change the advice you need. I will send you our ‘Support When You Need It Most’ brochure, which explains the help available. If you feel you need extra support at any point, please let me know.”



4. Needs Summary, Recommendation and Quote

Aim

Summarise the client’s main risks, confirm they agree, then explain your recommendation and quote clearly.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE!  MUST BE READ WORD FOR WORD!

“Based on what we have discussed, I see the areas of concern are:

[RECOMMENDATION]

Have I got that right?”

There needs to be a clear yes from the client before continuing.

Then explain:

Product or products.

Insurer.

Cover amount.

Term or policy length.

Premium.

Why it fits the client’s circumstances.









5. Agreement to Move into Health & Lifestyle

Aim

Before starting Health & Lifestyle questions, confirm the client understands what they are applying for.

If you are moving straight into the application with the recommendation above, you do not need to recap it. If this is a new call or the recommendation has changed, complete the recap again.

Confirm:

Product or products.

Insurer.

Cover amount.

Term.

Premium.

Why it has been recommended.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE!  MUST BE READ WORD FOR WORD!

“As mentioned, it is really important that all the questions I ask you today are answered honestly and accurately. In the event of a claim, the insurer could write to your GP and any inaccuracies could invalidate the policy or affect the claim.

At some points, providers may need to contact your GP for a medical report. This could be to assess a claim, or some providers do a 1 in 10 check on applications to make sure everything is as accurate as can be.

If they need to contact your GP, are you happy for them to do so?

And if they did, do you want to see a copy of the report before it’s sent to the provider?”

Then move into the Health & Lifestyle questions.
