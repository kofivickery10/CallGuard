Looking for a Balto alternative?

Balto is a real-time guidance platform: live playbooks, prompts and checklists on the agent's screen while the call happens. CallGuard AI is a compliance scoring product with a live breach-detection layer, built UK-first. They overlap on "real-time", but they solve different problems. This page is the honest comparison.

Where Balto wins

Balto is genuinely good at in-the-moment guidance.

Balto more or less defined the real-time guidance category, and for the job of steering agents while they speak it is a credible leader. Four things they do well:

Live on-screen playbooks

Dynamic checklists, objection-handling prompts and script guidance that update as the conversation unfolds. If your goal is changing agent behaviour mid-call at scale, this is Balto's home turf.

Real-time supervisor alerting

Supervisors see live calls that need attention and can coach in the moment. Mature workflows for barge-in and escalation across large floors.

Broad language support

Live guidance across a long list of languages and accents, which matters for multilingual floors and offshore operations.

US contact-centre pedigree

Strong references in US insurance, healthcare, collections and sales floors, with playbooks framed around US regimes such as TCPA and mini-Miranda. If that is your regulatory world, the fit is natural.

Where it falls short

Guidance is not evidence.

Balto's centre of gravity is prompting the agent. A UK compliance director's problem is proving to the FCA what actually happened on every call. Those are different products:

QA scoring is secondary to guidance

Balto scores calls against its playbooks, but the product is organised around steering behaviour, not producing per-criterion pass/fail verdicts with transcript evidence. CallGuard AI's output is built for the audit file: every verdict carries the quote that proved it.

US regulatory centre of gravity

Balto's compliance framing leads with TCPA, mini-Miranda and US healthcare. Consumer Duty's four outcomes, COBS 9.2 suitability, FG21/1 vulnerability and CONC forbearance are not the native vocabulary. UK-regulated firms end up translating.

Enterprise sales motion, custom pricing

No self-serve sign-up and no published pricing; buying Balto means a sales cycle sized for 50+ seat floors. CallGuard AI publishes pricing from £199 per seat, per month, and you can be scoring calls the same day you sign up.

No compliance-corrections learning loop

Balto improves by humans editing playbooks. CallGuard AI improves by your compliance officer correcting verdicts: each correction feeds the next batch of scoring, so the AI converges on your firm's interpretation of "suitable" and "fair value" automatically.

Alert philosophy: prompts vs breaches

Balto surfaces lots of in-call nudges by design. CallGuard AI's live layer deliberately fires only when the AI is over 75% confident a criterion has already failed, as an HMAC-signed webhook to your supervisor or CRM. Fewer alerts, each one actionable.

Retrospective scoring of recorded calls

Balto needs to be on the live call. CallGuard AI scores live streams and historic recordings: drag-and-drop files or push a recording URL via REST and re-score a back-book for an audit or a remediation exercise.

Who should pick which

Pick the right tool for your actual situation.

Pick Balto if

You run a 50+ seat floor, your primary goal is changing what agents say while they say it, your regulatory world is US-centric, and you have the budget and runway for an enterprise guidance rollout.

Pick CallGuard AI if

Your problem is evidencing compliance on 100% of calls to a UK regulator, you want per-criterion verdicts with transcript quotes, you need to score recorded calls as well as live ones, and you want published pricing and a same-day start rather than a procurement cycle.

FAQ

Common questions, answered.

Does CallGuard AI do real-time guidance like Balto?

Not today. CallGuard AI's live layer detects breaches mid-call and alerts a supervisor or your CRM within 30 seconds. A whisper-style in-call coach that nudges the agent directly is on our roadmap, scoped with design partners. If on-screen playbook guidance is your primary requirement right now, Balto is the more mature choice for that specific job.

Can we run CallGuard AI alongside Balto?

Yes. Some teams use a guidance tool on the live call and CallGuard AI as the compliance system of record: every call (guided or not) is scored against the scorecard, with evidence quotes, a breach register and coaching drafts. The two do not conflict; they listen to the same audio for different purposes.

How does pricing compare?

Balto quotes per deployment, typically for 50+ seat floors. CallGuard AI publishes pricing from £199 per seat, per month, with volume discounts and no multi-year minimum. Full details on the pricing page.

How do you handle FCA-regulated firms specifically?

That is a primary design point for CallGuard AI. Our scorecards lead with COBS 9.2 suitability, FG21/1 vulnerability, and the four Consumer Duty outcomes by default. More on financial services scoring here.
