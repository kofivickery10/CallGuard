Looking for a Convin alternative?

Convin is a broad conversation-intelligence suite: omnichannel audit, agent coaching, voice-of-customer analytics and virtual agents in one platform. CallGuard AI is a focused compliance call scoring product built UK-first for FCA-regulated conversations. This page is the honest comparison.

Where Convin wins

Convin is genuinely good at breadth.

Convin has built a wide conversation-intelligence platform, and if your requirement is one vendor covering every channel and every insight use case, it deserves a look. Four things they do well:

Omnichannel coverage

Convin audits calls, chats and emails in one place. If your QA remit spans live chat and email tickets as well as voice, that breadth is a real advantage over voice-only tools.

Automated coaching workflows

Coaching sessions are auto-assigned off the back of QA results, with peer-to-peer comparisons against your best performers. For large floors focused on ramping agents quickly, the coaching machinery is well developed.

Voice-of-customer analytics

Beyond QA, Convin mines conversations for customer sentiment, behaviour trends and winning phrases. If your buyer is a revenue or CX leader as much as a compliance one, that insight layer is part of the appeal.

Real-time agent assist

Live guidance and supervisor visibility during calls, plus AI virtual agents for inbound and outbound. A broad real-time suite if you want prompting as well as monitoring.

Where it falls short

Breadth is the wrong shape for a UK compliance problem.

Convin is built as a general conversation-intelligence suite with a global mid-market focus. For a UK firm whose actual problem is evidencing compliance to the FCA, that produces some gaps:

Compliance is a feature, not the product

Convin scores conversations for quality, sentiment and behaviour, with compliance as one lens among many. CallGuard AI is compliance-first: every verdict is a pass/fail against a scorecard item with a direct transcript quote as evidence, because that is what an auditor asks for.

No UK regulatory vocabulary

Consumer Duty's four outcomes, COBS 9.2 suitability, FG21/1 vulnerability, CONC forbearance, PECR consent: these are the terms a UK compliance director needs the tool to speak. Convin's framing is generic QA parameters; the mapping to FCA language is left to you.

Sales-led onboarding and custom quotes

Getting started means a demo, discovery and a custom quote. CallGuard AI is self-serve: sign up, upload a scorecard, score calls the same day, with pricing published from £199 per seat, per month.

UK data residency needs diligence

Convin serves a global market, so where your call audio and transcripts are processed and stored is a due-diligence question to ask. CallGuard AI is UK-hosted by default, with AES-256-GCM encryption at rest and a published UK GDPR Article 28 DPA and sub-processor list.

No compliance-corrections learning loop

CallGuard AI turns your compliance officer's one-click corrections into few-shot examples that recalibrate the next batch of scoring, so the AI converges on your firm's interpretation. Convin's coaching loop targets agent behaviour, not the scoring model itself.

Live alerts tuned for breaches, not prompts

Convin's real-time suite is guidance-oriented. CallGuard AI's live layer is breach-oriented: it only fires when the AI is over 75% confident a criterion has already failed, delivered as an HMAC-signed webhook to your supervisor or CRM, designed against alert fatigue.

FAQ

Common questions, answered.

Does CallGuard AI cover chat and email like Convin?

No. CallGuard AI is deliberately voice-first: live streams, dialler recordings and uploaded files. If omnichannel text QA is a hard requirement, Convin's breadth may fit better; some teams run CallGuard AI for regulated voice and a lighter tool for text channels.

Can CallGuard AI match Convin's coaching features?

CallGuard AI generates a coaching draft per scored call, and per-agent coaching memory means feedback builds on what was said to that agent last week rather than repeating a template. What we do not do is auto-assign training sessions or peer-comparison leagues; our coaching output is written for the human who runs your coaching conversations.

How does pricing compare?

Convin quotes per deployment. CallGuard AI publishes pricing from £199 per seat, per month, with volume discounts and no multi-year minimum. Full details on the pricing page.

How do you handle FCA-regulated firms specifically?

That is a primary design point for CallGuard AI. Our scorecards lead with COBS 9.2 suitability, FG21/1 vulnerability, and the four Consumer Duty outcomes by default. More on financial services scoring here.
